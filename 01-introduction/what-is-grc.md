# What is GRC in Cybersecurity

**Source:** Simply Cyber's Gerald Auger, PhD (YouTube)  
**Related Daily Log:** [Day 1](../00-learning-log/day-01-introduction.md)

---

## Executive Summary

GRC (Governance, Risk, and Compliance) is a structured approach to aligning IT with business objectives, while managing risk and meeting compliance requirements. Primarily implemented in middle-sized and large enterprises.

---

## The Three Pillars

### 1. Governance

**Definition:** The system by which an organization is directed and controlled.

**Implementation:**
- Rules and regulations
- Policies and procedures
- Organizational structure
- Cultural integration

**Key Concept:** Governance creates the **framework** for decision-making. It's not just documents—it's the **culture** of how decisions are made.

---

### 2. Risk

**Definition:** The effect of uncertainty on objectives.

**Scope:** The largest component of GRC.

**Assessment Methods:**

#### Qualitative Risk Assessment
- **Approach:** Categorical (High, Medium, Low)
- **Characteristics:** Subjective, experience-based
- **Advantages:** Quick, intuitive, low resource requirements
- **Disadvantages:** Inconsistent, hard to compare, potential bias

#### Quantitative Risk Assessment
- **Approach:** Numerical (dollar values, percentages)
- **Characteristics:** Data-driven, objective
- **Advantages:** Defensible, comparable, board-friendly
- **Disadvantages:** Requires mature metrics, complex calculations, resource-intensive

&gt; **Fundamental Truth:** 100% security is impossible. Risk management optimizes, not eliminates.

---

### 3. Compliance

**Definition:** Adherence to regulatory requirements, standards, and internal policies.

**Nature:** Minimum set of standards required to:
- Meet legal obligations
- Maintain certifications
- Avoid penalties and restrictions

**Compliance ≠ Security**
- Compliance is the **floor** (minimum requirement)
- Security is the **ceiling** (continuous improvement)

---

## Key Frameworks: NIST Publications

### NIST SP 800-39: Managing Information Security Risk

**Purpose:** Provides guidance on **how to implement** an organization-wide risk management framework.

**Scope:** Enterprise-level, integrated approach.

### NIST SP 800-37: Risk Management Framework (RMF)

**Purpose:** The actual framework for managing security and privacy risk.

**Components:**
1. Prepare
2. Categorize
3. Select
4. Implement
5. Assess
6. Authorize
7. Monitor

## The Audit & Remediation Cycle

Audit → Test → Score → Identify Gaps → Remediate → Report


**Critical Steps Often Missed:**

1. **Testing:** Audits without testing verify documentation, not reality
2. **Scoring:** Quantify findings for prioritization
3. **Action Planning:** Specific, measurable remediation steps
4. **Management Reporting:** Escalation with context and recommendations

**Common Failure Modes:**
- Controls misconfigured (technical)
- Users bypassing controls (human)
- Policy-procedure mismatch (process)

## Best Practices

### Policy Development

❌ **Anti-Pattern:** Writing 400 policies for compliance checkbox

✅ **Best Practice:**
1. Identify **critical** policies for your organization
2. Write **minimum viable** versions
3. **Communicate** throughout organization
4. Secure **top management buy-in**
5. **Enforce** consistently
6. **Review** regularly

### Critical Success Factors

| Factor | Why It Matters |
|--------|----------------|
| Executive Support | Resources and authority |
| Cultural Fit | Adoption and adherence |
| Proportionality | Right-sized for organization |
| Continuous Improvement | Adaptation to change |

---

## Key Takeaways

1. GRC scales with organization size and complexity
2. Risk assessment is the technical core
3. Governance provides the structure
4. Compliance provides the baseline
5. Effectiveness requires culture, not just documents
